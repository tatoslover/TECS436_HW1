A Brief History of NZ Currency

Pre-1840s
MƒÅori used barter, koha, and utu as forms of exchange ‚Äî goods, gifts, and reciprocal obligation rather than money.
1840s‚Äì1930s
British pounds, shillings, and pence used after colonisation. A confusing system with 12 pence to a shilling and 20 shillings to a pound.
1934
The Reserve Bank of New Zealand established, taking over the role of issuing currency.
1967
NZ goes decimal ‚Äî dollars and cents replace pounds and shillings. Much easier to calculate!
1990
NZ switches to polymer (plastic) notes ‚Äî longer lasting, harder to counterfeit, and more secure than paper.
1990s‚Äì2006
The 1c, 2c, 5c coins retired. Prices rounded to the nearest 10 cents for cash transactions.
2015‚Äì16
New series of notes issued with enhanced security features ‚Äî improved holograms, colour-shift ink, and updated portraits.
Today
Cash use is declining as contactless payments, EFTPOS, and digital banking become the norm ‚Äî but cash remains legal tender and essential for many.
